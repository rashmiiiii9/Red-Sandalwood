Red Sandalwood Smuggling

What is Red Sandalwood?

Red sandalwood, known as Pterocarpus santalinus, is a valuable hardwood used for medicinal, cosmetic, and decorative purposes. Its deep red hue makes it highly sought after, particularly in East Asian markets.

The Smuggling Problem

Due to high demand and strict legal controls, illegal trade has flourished. Smugglers harvest sandalwood from protected forests, leading to deforestation and loss of biodiversity.

Environmental Impact

Beyond financial crimes, smuggling devastates forest ecosystems. Native wildlife loses habitat, and forest regeneration slows drastically.

Economic & Social Impact

Illegal networks profit while local communities remain impoverished. Law enforcement faces significant challenges in combating this lucrative black market.
